01 Pre-Market Preparation 8:45 AM – 9:15 AM
Calculate Pivot Points
Use yesterday's High, Low, Close from NSE website or Zerodha Kite. Calculate P, R1, R2, S1, S2 for all 5 stocks. Use the calculator in Section 04. Mark these as horizontal lines on your TradingView chart before market opens.
Check Market Context
Check SGX Nifty / Gift Nifty for gap direction. Read any overnight news on your 5 stocks. Check if any stock has earnings, board meeting, or ex-dividend today — avoid trading those stocks on event days.
Set Up TradingView Charts
Open 15-min chart for each stock. Confirm these indicators are visible:

9 EMA  21 EMA  ADX (14)  Volume + 20 MA

Add 5 horizontal lines at pivot levels: S2, S1, P, R1, R2. Use different colors (red for S, gold for P, green for R).
Note Previous Day Range
Calculate each stock's previous day High − Low range. If range is below ₹30, skip that stock today — insufficient volatility for meaningful intraday moves. Log this in your journal before market opens. Saves you from trading dead stocks.
⚠ On high-impact days — RBI Policy, Union Budget, US Fed announcements, quarterly earnings for your stocks — either sit out completely or reduce position size to 50% and use wider stops. News-driven gaps will invalidate pivot levels.
02 The 4 Conditions — All Must Be Met Zero exceptions. Skip the trade if any one fails.
◈ This system's edge comes entirely from requiring ALL 4 conditions simultaneously. Removing even one condition — especially ADX or Volume — drops the win rate below breakeven. The conditions are the strategy.
CONDITION 01
EMA Crossover — Trend Direction
For Long: 9 EMA has crossed above 21 EMA on the 15-min chart, within the last 3 candles. OR price has pulled back to the 21 EMA and bounced without closing below it.

For Short: 9 EMA has crossed below 21 EMA. OR price bounced down from the 21 EMA after being below it.
9 EMA / 21 EMA · 15-min
CONDITION 02
Pivot Level — Price Trigger
For Long: Price closes above the Pivot (P), above R1, or bounces from S1 and closes back above P. The full 15-min candle must close above the level — wicks through don't count.

For Short: Price closes below P, below S1, or rejects from R1 and closes below P.
P · R1 · R2 · S1 · S2
CONDITION 03
ADX Reading — Trend Strength
ADX(14) value must be above 20 at the time of entry candle close. This confirms the market is in a trending mode, not ranging.

For Long: +DI line must be above -DI line.
For Short: -DI line must be above +DI line.

ADX below 20 = choppy market. No trade regardless of EMA or pivot.
ADX(14) > 20 · +DI/-DI check
CONDITION 04
Volume Spike — Confirmation
Volume on the signal candle must be at least 1.5× the 20-period average volume. This confirms institutional participation, not retail noise.

Hover over the volume bar on TradingView — the value shows in top-left. Compare to the orange MA line. Bar must be visibly ~50% taller than the MA line.

Low volume breakout = trap. No exceptions.
Vol ≥ 1.5× · 20-bar MA

04 Entry, Stop-Loss & Target Rules
▲ LONG (BUY) SETUP
ParameterRule
EntryMarket buy at open of next candle after all 4 conditions confirmed on candle close. Never enter mid-candle.
Stop-LossBelow S1 pivot level OR below the signal candle's low — whichever is closer to entry. Maximum stop = 0.5% of stock price.
Target 1R1 level. Book 60% of position here. Move stop to breakeven on remaining.
Target 2R2 level. Trail stop to previous candle low on remaining 40%.
Time StopIf trade doesn't move 0.3% in your favour within 3 candles (45 min), exit at market. Dead money.
▼ SHORT (SELL) SETUP
ParameterRule
EntryMarket sell at open of next candle after all 4 conditions confirmed. Short sell requires MIS margin in Zerodha Kite.
Stop-LossAbove R1 pivot level OR above signal candle's high — whichever is closer. Maximum stop = 0.5% of stock price.
Target 1S1 level. Cover 60% of short position here. Move stop to breakeven on rest.
Target 2S2 level. Trail stop to previous candle high on remaining 40%.
Time StopSame rule — no movement in 45 min = exit. Also exit if ADX drops below 18 after entry.

Trade Example — RELIANCE Long Setup
Pre-market: Calculated pivot levels. RELIANCE Prev H=2870, L=2820, C=2855. P=2848.33 · R1=2876.66 · S1=2826.66
9:30 AM: Market opens. Both 9 EMA and 21 EMA are flat. ADX = 16 (below 20). No setup. Watching only.
10:15 AM candle: 9 EMA crosses above 21 EMA. Price closes at ₹2,851 — above Pivot (₹2,848). ADX now = 23 with +DI above -DI. Volume bar = 1,18,000 vs 20-bar MA of 72,000 → ratio = 1.64×. All 4 conditions met.
10:30 AM candle open: Enter buy at ₹2,853. Stop-loss below S1 = ₹2,826 (stop = ₹27). Target 1 = R1 = ₹2,876 (profit = ₹23). Target 2 = R2 = ₹2,898 (profit = ₹45). R:R = 1 : 1.67 at T1 — acceptable.
11:15 AM: Price hits R1 at ₹2,876. Exit 60% of position (+₹23/share). Move stop to ₹2,851 (breakeven) on remaining 40%.
12:00 PM: Price reaches ₹2,892 (near R2). Exit remaining 40% position. Total trade: blended average gain = ~₹32/share on full position.
05 Daily Trading Checklist Click each item to mark complete
◆ Before Market Opens (8:45–9:15 AM)
  • 8:45 AM Calculate pivots for all 5 stocks using prev day H/L/C. Mark lines on TradingView.
  • 8:50 AM Check Gift Nifty for gap direction. Note if gap > 0.5% — be extra cautious today.
  • 8:55 AM Scan for events today — earnings, RBI, results, dividends on your 5 stocks. Mark event stocks as "avoid today."
  • 9:00 AM Check previous day range (H−L) for each stock. Skip any stock with range < ₹30.
  • 9:05 AM Open TradingView 15-min charts for all 5 stocks. Confirm EMA(9,21), ADX(14), Volume(20MA) are loaded.
  • 9:10 AM Set up Zerodha Kite with watchlist. Know the lot size and margin required for each stock.
  • 9:12 AM Write down today's max loss limit in your journal before markets open. This is non-negotiable.
◆ During Market Hours
  • 9:15–9:30 Do nothing. Watch the opening move. No trades in first 15 min. Let gap fill and volatility settle.
  • Every candle At each 15-min candle close, scan all 5 stocks for setup formation. Run the 4-condition check mentally.
  • On setup Before entering: EMA ✓ · Pivot ✓ · ADX>20 ✓ · Volume≥1.5x ✓ — all four. If any one is missing, skip.
  • On entry Enter at next candle open only. Place bracket order with stop and target simultaneously.
  • Mid-session If ADX drops below 18 on any open position, exit that trade — trend is dying.
  • Max 3 trades Stop trading after 3 trades total per day across all stocks, or after daily loss limit is hit.
  • 3:10 PM Exit all open intraday positions by 3:10 PM. No exceptions. Square off in Kite if needed.
◆ After Market Closes (3:30–4:00 PM)
  • 3:35 PM Log all trades in journal — stock, time, entry, exit, SL, target, P&L, conditions met.
  • 3:40 PM Rate each trade on rule-following (1–5 stars) independent of whether it was a winner or loser. A losing trade with all rules followed = 5 stars.
  • 3:50 PM Calculate tomorrow's pivots using today's final H/L/C (available on NSE after 3:30 PM). Mark them for tomorrow.
  • Friday only Weekly review: Win rate this week, average R:R, which stocks performed best, any rule violations to correct.

08 Golden Rules — Dos & Don'ts
Always Do
01
Wait for all 4 conditions. Not 3. Not "almost 4." All four. Every. Single. Time.
02
Enter at candle open, not mid-candle. Chasing a candle that's already moved 0.3% eliminates your R:R advantage.
03
Book 60% at T1 (R1 or S1). Protecting partial profits removes emotion from the remaining position.
04
Square off by 3:10 PM daily. Intraday positions held to close carry overnight risk on an intraday setup.
05
Journal every trade same day, while it's fresh. Note why you took it and what you'd do differently.
06
Sit out on event days. News releases, RBI policy, budget — pivot levels become meaningless when price gaps.
Never Do
01
Never average down on a losing trade. If your stop is hit, the trade is over. Adding to a loser kills accounts.
02
Never trade after hitting your daily loss limit. Revenge trading after losses is the single biggest cause of account blowups.
03
Never hold intraday positions overnight hoping for a gap-up. This is a day trading system. All positions close same day.
04
Never trade the first 15 minutes (9:15–9:30 AM). Opening prices are manipulated by large players — unreliable for technical signals.
05
Never take more than 3 trades per day. Overtrading is just gambling with extra steps. Your edge requires patience.
06
Never skip the volume check because "the setup looks perfect." Low-volume breakouts at pivot levels are the most common retail trap in Indian markets.
★
The most important rule of all: A system with a 55% win rate only works if you take every valid setup and skip every invalid one with equal discipline. Selective rule-following — taking setups that "feel right" and skipping those that "feel off" — destroys the statistical edge entirely. Trust the system, not your gut, for the first 6 months.
09 TradingView Pine Script — Volume Spike Indicator
Paste this in TradingView Pine Editor → Add to Chart
//@version=5
indicator("Volume Spike 1.5x", overlay=false)
vol_ma = ta.sma(volume, 20)
is_spike = volume >= vol_ma * 1.5
plot(vol_ma, color=color.orange, linewidth=2, title="20-bar Avg Volume")
plot(volume, style=plot.style_columns, color=is_spike ? color.new(color.green, 20) : color.new(color.gray, 60))
Volume bars turn bright green when the 1.5× spike condition is met. Gray bars = below threshold = skip the trade. OHLC candle colors remain unchanged.
